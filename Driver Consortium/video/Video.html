Video
Millennium supports all standard VGA cards, however, support of SVGA cards is limited due to the lack of international standards. As a result of this lack of a standard each video card has to have it's own driver to modify the cards features at a register level. It is hoped that video card manufactures will in future directly provide support by developing there own Millennium drivers for their cards. Until this is done, however, high resolution video drivers will have to be developed soley by the Millennium development team and individual Millennium users.

The Millennium Operating System provides a generic video card interface between the kernel and the video driver. This interface is known as the Millennium Video Abstraction Layer (MVAL).
