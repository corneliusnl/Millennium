Mouse

The mouse interface is quite simple, with the mouse periodically checking its position and button status and sending a packet to the serial port. Each byte received is stored in the serial port receive buffer. Having placed the byte in the buffer, the serial port controller issues an interrupt - IRQ4 for COM1. If that interrupt is not masked eithor in the serial port or in the interrupt controller, it arrives at the processor and the INT 0Ch (for COM1) occurs.

Two Button Mice

For 2 buttom mice this packet consists of three bytes.

Byte 1

76543210
        +---- Bit 6 of x coordinate
       +----- Bit 7 of x coordinate
      +------ Bit 6 of y coordinate
     +------- Bit 7 of y coordinate
    +-------- Right button (If 1 ? down : up)
   +--------- Left button (If 1 ? down : up)
  +---------- Always 1
 +-----------

Byte 2

Bit 0-5	0-5 bits of X coordinate
Bit 6	Always 0

Byte 3

Bit 0-5	0-5 bits of Y coordinate
Bit 6	Always 0
